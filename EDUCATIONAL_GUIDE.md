## What You'll Learn

### 1. The Moon Doesn't Glow
The Moon reflects sunlight‚Äîit doesn't produce its own light. This is why we see different phases.

### 2. The Sun Always Illuminates Half the Moon
Just like Earth always has a day side and night side, the Moon always has half of its surface lit by the Sun.

### 3. Our Perspective Changes
As the Moon orbits Earth, we see different portions of its illuminated half. This creates the phases.

### 4. It's All About Geometry
The phases are purely geometric‚Äîno magic, no conspiracy, just the positions of three spherical objects in space.

## The 8 Main Lunar Phases

### New Moon (0¬∞)
- Moon is between Earth and Sun
- Dark side faces Earth
- Moon rises and sets with the Sun (not visible at night)

### Waxing Crescent (45¬∞)
- Small sliver of light on right side
- Visible in evening sky after sunset

### First Quarter (90¬∞)
- Right half illuminated
- Visible from noon to midnight
- Called "quarter" because it's 1/4 of the way through the cycle

### Waxing Gibbous (135¬∞)
- More than half lit, growing toward full
- Visible most of the night

### Full Moon (180¬∞)
- Moon opposite the Sun (Earth in middle)
- Fully illuminated face toward Earth
- Rises at sunset, sets at sunrise

### Waning Gibbous (225¬∞)
- More than half lit, shrinking
- Visible late evening through morning

### Last Quarter (270¬∞)
- Left half illuminated
- Visible from midnight to noon

### Waning Crescent (315¬∞)
- Small sliver on left side
- Visible in morning sky before sunrise

## Key Concepts for Flat Earth Skeptics

### The Phases Prove Spherical Geometry

1. **Consistent Pattern**: The phases follow a predictable 29.5-day cycle that matches orbital mechanics
2. **Light Direction**: The illuminated part always points toward the Sun's position
3. **No Earth Shadow**: Most phases are NOT caused by Earth's shadow (only lunar eclipses are)
4. **Visible from Everywhere**: The same phase is visible from all locations on Earth at the same time

### What About Lunar Eclipses?

During a lunar eclipse:
- Earth IS between the Sun and Moon
- Earth's ROUND shadow passes across the Moon
- This proves Earth is spherical
- Eclipses are different from phases

### Observable Facts

- **The Moon orbits Earth**: Takes ~27.3 days (sidereal month)
- **The phase cycle**: Takes ~29.5 days (synodic month - longer because Earth moves too)
- **The tilt**: Moon's orbit is tilted ~5¬∞ (why we don't get eclipses every month)
- **Same face**: The Moon is tidally locked (same side always faces Earth)

## Advanced Topics

### Why 29.5 Days Instead of 27.3?

The Moon orbits Earth in 27.3 days (sidereal month), but Earth is also orbiting the Sun. So the Moon needs an extra ~2 days to "catch up" to the same Sun-Earth-Moon alignment. This is the synodic month (29.5 days).

### Libration

The Moon appears to wobble slightly, letting us see ~59% of its surface over time (not just 50%). This simulation uses a simplified tidally-locked rotation, but real-world libration is more complex.

### Why No Eclipse Every Month?

The Moon's orbit is tilted ~5¬∞ relative to Earth's orbit around the Sun. So most months, the Moon passes "above" or "below" Earth's shadow at Full Moon, preventing an eclipse.
